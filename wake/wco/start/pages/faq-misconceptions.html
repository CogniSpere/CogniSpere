FAQ & Misconceptions

Is this a government?

No. The WCO has no governing authority. It is a neutral civic commons.

Is this a protest platform?

No. Participation does not require conflict. Praise, questions, silence, or observation are equally valid.

Does participation mean opposition?

No. Contributions may support, critique, or simply observe institutions and policies.

Do I need permission or membership?

No. Anyone may contribute. There is no membership, approval process, or gatekeeping.

Are there leaders?

No formal leaders exist. Visibility comes from participation, not hierarchy.

Can governments ignore this?

Yes. But their silence is also part of the civic record. Presence and absence both matter.

What if my issue is small?

Scale does not matter. A neighborhood concern is as valid as a planetary issue.

Does WCO override sovereignty?

No. WCO witnesses alongside governments; it does not replace or override them.

WCO: Presence is valid, even if misunderstood.
